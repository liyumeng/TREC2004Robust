<!-- PJG ITAG l=10 g=1 f=2 -->
SUMMARY:
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=10 g=1 f=1 -->
 This proposed rule revises the Agricultural Stabilization and Conservation Service (ASCS) regulations governing
the Agricultural Conservation Program (ACP), Emergency Conservation Program (ECP), and the Forestry Incentives
Program (FIP). This proposed rule incorporates amendments made to the ACP, ECP, and FIP; revises procedures to reflect
current policies; and incorporates the Water Quality Incentive Projects (WQIP) into the ACP.

<!-- PJG ITAG l=84 g=1 f=2 -->
Background 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=2 -->
The ACP is authorized generally by Sections 7&hyph;17 of the Soil Conservation and Domestic Allotment Act as amended
(the Act) (16 U.S.C. 22590g 
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=3 -->
et seq.
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=1 -->
). The program provides financial and technical assistance to encourage agricultural producers to voluntarily
perform enduring soil and water conservation, water quality, and pollution abatement measures, including practices
or programs which are deemed essential to maintain soil productivity, prevent soil depletion, maintain water quality,
or prevent increased cost of production. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
A WQIP is authorized by the Food, Agriculture, Conservation, and Trade Act of 1990 (16 U.S.C. 3831 
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=3 -->
et seq.
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=1 -->
). However, Congress funded WQIP as a part of the ACP. Accordingly, the WQIP is a voluntary incentive program conducted
pursuant to the Act to develop and implement 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
agricultural water quality protection plans on up to 10 million acres. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG STAG 4702 -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=1 -->
The ECP is authorized by the Agricultural Credit Act of 1978 (16 U.S.C. 2201 
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=3 -->
et seq.
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=1 -->
). This program is designed to provide cost-share assistance for emergency restoration work to meet only the critical
needs of agricultural producers due to drought or other natural disaster. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
The FIP is authorized by section 4 of the Cooperative Forestry Assistance Act of 1978 (16 U.S.C. 2103) and is designed
to increase the nation's supply of timber products from private nonindustrial forest lands. The purpose of FIP is
to encourage private landowners to apply forestry practices that will provide for afforestation of suitable open
lands and reforestation of cut-over or other nonstocked forest lands and to encourage intensive multi-purpose forest
resource management and protection so as to provide for cost-effective timber production and other related forest
resources needs. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
The regulation governing these programs is being restructured and simplified to provide an easier and more effective
structure for the use of this regulation. The ACP is being revised to: 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(1) Incorporate several amendments that have been published in the 
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=4 -->
Federal Register
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=1 -->
; 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(2) Incorporate the WQIP; 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(3) Clarify that producers wanting to participate with practice WP4, Agricultural Waste Control Facilities, must
have been in operation for at least 5 years; 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(4) Adjust the LTA payment limitation to reflect that the payment limitation shall be based on the number of years remaining
on the LTA; and 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(5) reflect that payments shall be based on attribution. The ECP is being revised to: 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(1) Reflect that the Disaster Assistance Act of 1989 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(16 U.S.C. 2202) authorized assistance for confined livestock operations; and 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(2) Define land subject to frequent damage which is ineligible for cost-share assistance. 
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG QTAG 02 -->
<!-- PJG /QTAG -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=10 g=1 f=1 -->
The FIP is being restructured for clarity. 
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG QTAG 02 -->
<!-- PJG /QTAG -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=1 -->
Currently, ACP practices are prioritized locally by county Agricultural Stabilization and Conservation committees
to ensure that the most severe conservation and environmental problems are addressed. However, this county committee
prioritization process has been criticized as not addressing the most severe conservation and environmental problems.
Therefore, comments are requested regarding the manner in which ACP practices and allocation of funds could be prioritized
to achieve greater conservation and environmental benefits at the least Federal cost. Although not exclusive, such
considerations for prioritization may be based on temporary versus longer-term practices or type of conservation
or environmental benefit. Further, consideration should be given to whether priorities should be established by
the National, State, or county Agricultural Stabilization and Conservation Service offices. Moreover, for ACP,
ECP and FIP, comments are requested on ways in which the programs can best be targeted in order to ensure that public
benefits, rather than private benefits are maximized with Federal expenditures. For example, the use of ACP for temporary
practices may not provide large public benefits. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
Comments are requested as to whether funds should be targeted toward designated areas with impaired water quality.
Similarly, cost-share practices could be selected for a region where they will contribute to an identified problem.
Thus, nutrient management practices would be designated as a cost-share option in areas where nutrients have been
identified as a water quality problem.

<!-- PJG ITAG l=80 g=1 f=1 -->
&sect;701.1 Background. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=1 -->
(a) Through the conservation and environmental programs administered by the Department of Agriculture, the Federal
Government will share with farmers, ranchers, and other eligible private landowners in the United States and the
applicable territories and possessions of the United States, the cost of performing: 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(1) Approved soil and water conservation, water quality, and pollution abatement practices including related wildlife
conservation practices. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(2) Emergency conservation measures. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(3) Approved forestry practices. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(b) The Federal Government will provide incentive payments to farmers, ranchers, and other private landowners in
applicable areas of the United States to establish Water Quality Incentive Projects (WQIP) practices. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(c) Cost-share assistance or incentive payments may be made available to eligible program participants by the Agricultural
Stabilization and Conservation Service (ASCS) for: 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(1) Soil and water conservation, water quality, and pollution abatement practices under the Agricultural Conservation
Program (ACP) which includes WQIP. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(2) Practices to correct damage to land or conservation practices caused by natural disaster under the Emergency
Conservation Program (ECP). 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(3) Installation of water conservation measures under the ECP during periods of severe drought. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(4) Forestry practices under the ACP and the Forestry Incentives Program (FIP). 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(d) Information on the practices for which costs will be shared, the exact specifications and rates of cost-share
assistance for such practices, and the eligibility 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
requirements for participating in the programs, may be obtained from the County Agricultural Stabilization and
Conservation (ASC) Committee (county committee) for the county in which the farm, ranch, or other eligible land is
administered or from the State ASC committee (State committee) for the State in which such county is located. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(e) The provisions at subpart A apply to the programs specified in subparts B through D. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=80 g=1 f=1 -->
&sect;701.2
<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=89 g=1 f=1 -->
Administration. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG /ITAG -->

<!-- PJG ITAG l=11 g=1 f=1 -->
(a) The regulations in this part will be administered under the general supervision of the Administrator, ASCS and
shall be carried out in the field by State and county Agricultural Stabilization and Conservation committees (State
and county ASC committees). 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(b) State and county ASC committees, and representatives and employees thereof do not have the authority to modify
or waive any of the provisions of the regulations of this part. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(c) The State ASC committee shall take any action required by these regulations which has not been taken by the county
ASC committee. The State ASC committee shall also: 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(1) Correct, or require a county ASC committee to correct any action taken by such county ASC committee which is not
in accordance with the regulations of this part, or 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(2) Require a county ASC committee to withhold taking any action which is not in accordance with the regulations of
this part. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->

<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
(d) No provision or delegation herein to a State or county ASC committee shall preclude the Administrator, ASCS, or
a designee, from determining any question arising under the regulations of this part or from reversing or modifying
any determination made by a State or county ASC committee. 
<!-- PJG 0012 frnewline -->
